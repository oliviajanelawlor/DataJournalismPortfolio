Councillor Alex Aitken praises Birmingham Suicide Prevention Strategy for “breaking barriers
regarding mental health”

Photo credits: Elliott Brown from Birmingham, United Kingdom

Birmingham City Council have agreed to give their support of a new Suicide Prevention Scheme Strategy for the city.

The strategy has been co-produced with the council and a wide range of organisations as a shared approach to reducing deaths through suicide.

Provisional data published by the Office of National Statistics show that in the first three quarters of 2019, 400 people died by suicide, an 8% increase from the first three quarters of 2018.

Men suffered the largest increase with 31 more men dying by suicide in the first three quarters of 2019 to 2018.



At the council meeting, Councillor Simon Morrall said: “There is a crisis of masculinity in this country, but nobody wants to talk about it.

“The majority of suicides are men. Men make up a lot of domestic violence, rape and sexual violence statistics. We need to start treating people as people and put identity in the bin.”


The Suicide Prevention Strategy sets out a series of key priority areas for action across the partnership:
  • Reducing the risk of suicide in high-risk groups
  • Improving mental health in specific groups
  • Reducing access to means of suicide
  • Provide better information and support to those bereaved or affected by suicide
  • Support the media in delivering sensitive approaches to suicide and suicidal behaviour
  • Support research, data collection and monitoring


    • Councillor Alex Aitken strongly supported the strategy and thanked the councillors that have broken barriers for talking about suicides and mental health.

    He also supported the priority within the strategy to support the media in reporting on suicide and suicidal behaviour, saying:

    “Too many articles release specific and excessive details on those that have taken their lives. Our media has a huge responsibility to care for its sources. I do not think the dramatic impact targeted for the reader is justified for the effects for the families and those themselves.

    “If you are a journalist, please do look at these guidelines.”

    Samaritans have     media guidelines for journalists reporting on stories regarding suicide.

    In 2018, the occupation with the highest number of men dying by suicide in the UK was construction and building trades, followed by road transport drivers.



    In the same year, the occupation with the highest number of women dying by suicide was women working in caring personal services.



    Liberal Democrat Councillor Paul Tilsley raised the issue of long waiting times to seek treatment for a mental health issue:
    “If you go along to your GP in the morning, 25% of the people there have a mental problem, not a physical one. We have to recognise that it is an issue collectively. We need to equal and prioritise mental health, so nobody takes their own life.”


    Cabinet Member for Health and Social Care Paulette Hamilton, who forwarded the motion, assured members that “it’s not a strategy that is left on the shelf.

    “A strategy is a book of pages that we need to wait to come to life. I can assure you that it will not just be a document left on the shelf, its a piece of work that we need to work on together as a system.”
